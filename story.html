Jinx's Origin Story

Before she became Jinx, she was Powder, a timid and bright-eyed girl growing up in the shadows of Zaun, a city suffocated beneath the wealth and control of Piltover. She was not born as chaos. She was shaped into it. She grew up alongside her older sister Vi, holding onto the belief that she could belong somewhere and be useful. She wanted to be strong in a world that rarely gave her space to grow. But in a place defined by inequality and violence, even small mistakes carried heavy consequences. One moment changed everything and nothing was ever the same again. After loss and separation, Powder was taken in by Silco, a man driven by his own vision of revolution. He gave her a new name, Jinx. Under his influence, her pain was not healed. It was redirected. Her fragile emotions turned into unstable energy, and her creativity became more destructive. Her inventions grew dangerous, her behavior unpredictable, and her sense of identity slowly fractured under pressure from memory and trauma.

Once the most wanted criminal in Zaun, Jinx became something more than a fugitive. She became a symbol of chaos that no one could fully control. To some, she represents rebellion against oppression and the anger of the forgotten. To others, she is a warning of what happens when a broken system creates broken people and leaves them to rebuild themselves alone. She is not a traditional hero. She does not fight for peace or order. Yet in the eyes of those who feel abandoned by the world above, her chaos feels like truth. At the center of everything remains a single reality that never fully disappears. Jinx was once Powder. And somewhere beneath the noise, the laughter, and the destruction, that part of her still exists.
